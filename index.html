About Me

I'm a mechanical engineer with a minor in computer science, graduated from Northeastern University in May 2026. My background spans R&D at consumer electronics, semiconductor equipment, and energy-storage startups — with three co-ops across SharkNinja, Entegris, and PolyJoule Inc.

I work across the full product-development cycle: concept, CAD, prototype, test, iterate. I'm also comfortable on the software side — at SharkNinja I built a Python-based tool that replaced qualitative gut-checks with a quantitative scoring system for heat distribution, which the team adopted as their standard R&D workflow.

Outside of work I'm building Aphrones — a convertible headphone system designed around hairstyles that traditional headphones can't accommodate. It's a real hardware problem I'm solving from scratch: mechanism design, human factors, and manufacturing feasibility.
